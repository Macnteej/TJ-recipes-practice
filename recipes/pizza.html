Pizza

Description

Ingredients

  • 3 1/2 cups of flour
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water (95-105 degrees f)
  • 1/2 TBSP sugar
  • 1 TSP salt
  • 2 1/4 TBSP Active Dry Yeast
  • 8 ounces of tomato sauce
  • 8 ounces of shredded mozzarella
  • toppings of your choosing
  • Optional: 1 TBSP olive oil

Steps for making dough

  1. In a bowl or standmixer combine your flour, sugar, salt, and optional olive oil. Begin mixing on low speed to stir ingredients together.
  2. In a separate container, begin mixing your 1 1/2 cups of water with your yeast. Allow yeast to bloom for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Once the yeast has bloomed pour the mixture into the dry ingredients. Begin mixing until the dough begins to form a ball.
  4. Once dough is formed, lightly flour a work surface and begin kneading the dough until it begins to become smooth
  5. Once smooth, lather the dough with Olive oil and place in a mixing bowl 2.5 times its size and cover with plastic wrap. Allow to rise for 6 hours, or overnight at room temperature (inside of an oven that is off with the oven light on is best)
  6. Once it has doubled in size, push down on the center of the dough and deflate the ball. Pour onto a lightly floured work surface and begin to separate the dough into 4 even pieces.
  7. Roll the 4 pieces individually into new dough balls
  8. Once cut and rolled, either put in containers for freezing, for refrigeration for the next day, or let rise for one hour before making the pizza.

Steps to make pizza

  1. Begin by preheating your oven to the highest temperature it can go. Ideally at least 500 degrees with a baking stone for one hour.
  2. When the oven has preheated, dust your dough with flour and begin to stretch the dough to the size you want.
  3. Once stretched, place the stretched dough onto a pizza peel and begin to spread a layer of sauce onto the dough evenly.
  4. Once evenly spread, begin to sprinkle the cheese onto the pizza however thick or thin you desire.
  5. Once the cheese is spread begin to add the toppings of your choosing.
  6. Once ready to bake the pizza, give the peel a slight shake to confirm if it will slide off easily. If sticking, pick up an edge and lightly blow under the dough and sprinkle more flour underneath.
  7. When launching the pizza into the oven, aim for the center of the baking stone, and launch with one smooth push.
  8. Once on the stone, let pizza bake for 3-4 minutes and then rotate 180 degrees and bake for another 3-4 minutes
  9. When pulling the pizza out after 6-8 minutes, or when browned to your liking, let the pizza rest for 2 minutes before cutting
  10. With a pizza cutter or knife, slice the pizza into as many even slices as you want.
  11. Enjoy!
